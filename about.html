Our Purpose
Mission, Vision & Values
Every action we take is guided by three core commitments — rooted in Islamic values and driven by compassion.

Mission

To provide interest-free (0% riba) loans to low-income families, disbursed as goods or direct payments — never cash — so families can meet real needs without falling deeper into debt.

Vision

A Bangladesh where no family is forced to borrow at interest. We envision a revolving fund that grows with every repayment, reaching hundreds of households every year.

Values

Transparency in every transaction. Dignity for every borrower. Zero tolerance for riba. Trust earned through accountability — not promises.

Our Story
Began with a simple question

Korje Hasana Foundation began in 2026 with a question asked by a group of concerned community members: "What if we could help our neighbors borrow money without charging them interest?"

Inspired by the Quranic concept of Qard al-Hasan — the beautiful loan — we set out to build a revolving fund where every taka repaid becomes someone else's lifeline.

We follow the Bai-Muajjal principle: all loans are disbursed as goods or paid bills, never as cash. This ensures money reaches the intended purpose and protects borrowers from misuse.

Islamic Finance
Built on Shariah Principles
Every policy, every transaction, every decision at Korje Hasana is designed to align with Islamic financial ethics.

Zero Riba (0% Interest)

We charge absolutely zero interest on any loan. The borrower repays only the principal amount — nothing more, nothing less. Riba is categorically prohibited in Islam.

In-Kind Disbursement

Following Bai-Muajjal principles, funds are disbursed directly as goods, seeds, medicine, livestock or paid as vendor bills — never given as cash to the borrower.

Revolving Qard Hasan

Every repayment goes back into the fund pool and is lent to the next family in need. The fund grows with every act of charity, multiplying the benefit endlessly.

Full Transparency (Amanah)

Every taka in and out is publicly logged in our transparency dashboard. We are entrusted with donor funds and guard that trust with complete openness.

Shura (Collective Decision)

All loan approvals require a quorum of 3 out of 5 committee members. No single person holds unilateral authority over fund disbursement.

Sadaqah-Backed Model

Operating costs (5%) are covered by sadaqah, while 90% of donations go directly to the loan fund. The remaining 5% is held as an emergency reserve.
